Question 1
Your Answer
✔ Correct: Let's take a look at your phone settings.
Why? The customer just received this phone from Telecom — it is nearly new. Asking "Has anything changed recently?" is less useful here because the phone was never confirmed to be working with email. Since the device is new, the most logical step is to go directly to the phone's email settings and verify they are configured correctly. Casual or dismissive responses are unprofessional. Sending the user to a website guide without actively troubleshooting does not resolve the issue.
Question 2
Your Answer
✔ Correct: Please change the port number on your mail settings to 993.
Why? The settings screenshot shows the email account is using IMAP protocol with SSL encryption. The standard IMAP port is 143, but when SSL is enabled, IMAP must use port 993. The phone was set to port 100, which is incorrect. Port 25 is for SMTP (outgoing mail). Port 465 is SMTP with SSL. Port 143 is IMAP without SSL. Because SSL is active, port 993 is the correct choice.
Question 3
Your Answer
✔ Correct: B — Educate the user on the solution that was performed
Why? After resolving a help desk issue, best practice is to educate the end user about what was wrong and how it was fixed. This empowers them to recognize or even self-resolve similar issues in the future, and gives them context to relay to a help desk agent if the problem recurs. Closing the ticket immediately skips this important step. Telling the user to fix it themselves is unprofessional. Emailing Telecom may be a later step, but it is not the immediate next step after resolution.
