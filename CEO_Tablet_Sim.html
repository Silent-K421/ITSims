SIM 01

Simulation 1: Mobile Device Configuration

Scenario

Ann, a CEO, has purchased a new consumer-class tablet for personal use, but she is unable to connect to the wireless network. Other users have reported that their personal devices are connecting without issues. She has asked you to assist with getting the device online without adjusting her home WiFi configuration.

Instructions
  1. Review the network diagrams and device configurations to determine the cause of the problem and resolve any discovered issues.
  2. Click the WAP button on the floorplan to view the available wireless networks and their security settings.
  3. Click the Tablet in Office 3 to open the tablet's iOS settings.
  4. Configure the tablet's Wi-Fi settings with the correct SSID, 802.11 standard, frequency, security mode, and passphrase based on what you find in the WAP.
  5. Click Check Answers when done to see your results.

📖 Solution Walkthrough

  1. WAP Review: The WAP shows two SSIDs — CORP (WPA2) and BYOD (WPA-PSK). Since Ann is using a personal (consumer) device on the corporate network, she should connect to BYOD (Bring Your Own Device).
  2. Security Mode: The BYOD network uses WPA (WPA-PSK). WPA2 is for the CORP network only.
  3. Passphrase: The BYOD TotallySecure! column shows TotallySecure! as the password.
  4. Frequency: The WiFi Heat Map shows both 2.4GHz and 5GHz coverage, but when checked, 5GHz does not reach Office 3. Only 2.4GHz reaches Ann's desk in Office 3.
  5. 802.11 Standard: Since 2.4GHz is required, and the standard options are b/g, a, and ac — only b/g operates on the 2.4GHz band. (802.11a and 802.11ac use 5GHz.)
